Under the stochastic Heston framework, the previously successful static-skew CNN architecture failed to generalize without hyperparameter tuning or dataset scaling. The Residual CNN was unable to outperform the baseline ATM Black-Scholes model, producing structural errors (negative improvement %) on heavily correlated surfaces. This valid negative result proves that mapping stochastic PDEs requires a larger network capacity or a more robustly sampled training space than static deterministic skews.
