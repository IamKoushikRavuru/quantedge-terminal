2. Information Deficiency Analysis
----------------------------------
Why did the model fail systematically? 
The CNN input space is defined strictly as a 3-channel state: [Moneyness (K/S), Maturity (T), Normalized BS Price].
Under a deterministic, time-invariant skew model (Phase 4), this state is a sufficient statistic to map the residual. 
However, under the Heston model, the residual surface is NOT Markovian in (K, T) alone. 

The Heston residual PDE is driven heavily by the correlation parameter (ho) and the volatility of volatility (
u).
Because ho and 
u are NEVER passed to the CNN, the network is mathematically blind to the regime. 
It attempts to learn an 'average' correction across all random surfaces it sees during training, rather than a conditional correction.
Therefore, it structurally fails when evaluated on surfaces generated by aggressive parameter extremes.

3. Regime-Aware Design Proposal
-------------------------------
To generalize stochastic PDEs with ML, the system must become Regime-Aware.
Proposal: A 'Conditional Residual Corrector'.
- The input tensor must be expanded or conditioned on latent regime embeddings.
- (K/S, T) form the spatial grid.
- (
u, ho, v0, \kappa, 	heta) must be injected densely into the network (e.g., via FiLM layers or concatenated channels).
This ensures the ML model behaves as a true parameterized PDE solver rather than a blind interpolator.

4. The Boundary of ML and Theory
--------------------------------
What should NEVER be learned by ML?
1. Exponential payoffs and asymptotic boundaries (Deep ITM/OTM bounds).
2. The fundamental martingale probability measures (P1, P2 integrations in Heston).
Theory must remain the dominant engine enforcing no-arbitrage bounds. ML is strictly relegated to fitting the structurally complex integrations that are computationally expensive in live environments.
